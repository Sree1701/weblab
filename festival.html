INDIAN FESTIVAL

  • Diwali

    •

    Diwali, or Deepavali, is the Indian Festival of Lights, celebrating the triumph of good over evil and light over darkness. The five-day festival, observed by Hindus, Jains, and Sikhs, involves families cleaning and decorating their homes with oil lamps (diyas), rangoli, and lights. Festivities include worshipping Goddess Lakshmi, sharing sweets, exchanging gifts, and lighting fireworks

  • Onam

    •

    Onam is Kerala's major annual 10-day harvest festival, celebrating the homecoming of the benevolent King Mahabali with cultural festivities, traditional feasts, and the creation of flower carpets (Pookalams). This predominantly Hindu festival has evolved into a cultural celebration that brings together people of all faiths in Kerala and beyond, fostering unity, prosperity, and gratitude for the harvest season

  • Holi

    •

    Holi, or the "Festival of Colors," is an ancient Indian and Nepalese Hindu festival celebrating the arrival of spring, the triumph of good over evil, and the beginning of a new season of love and joy. Celebrated on the full moon day of the Hindu month of Phalgun (late February/early March), Holi involves a night of bonfires called Holika Dahan and a day of vibrant celebrations where people throw colored powders and water, sing, dance, and share festive sweets. The festival symbolizes forgiveness and the mending of relationships.
